2„ÄÅBimodal Iontronic Skins for Large-Area Robotic Coverage

Here, we develop a flexible bimodal skin with modular design for large-area coverage, integrating pressure and temperature sensing to enable intent-driven collaboration. We implement full-coverage skins on entire robotic arm, integrating over 768 pressure and 75 temperature sensing units, and develop a rapid, crosstalk-free readout interface based on a frequency-coding architecture. Our system allows subconscious touches at any location on the skin to be accurately interpreted in real time, enabling precise determination of human intent. We demonstrate that our technique facilitates smooth, adaptive interaction for individuals with mobility impairments, even under complex or emergency conditions. The human-centered interaction enabled by the large-area coverage, bimodal skin may be extended to many other applications.

Core Skills

  • Preparation of Iontronic Sensors. Proficient in micro-nano fabrication techniques for flexible sensors, such as maskless photolithography and magnetron sputtering, along with the integration of ion gel films.

  • Proficient in hardware circuit design, including PCB design, FPGA (Verilog) and ARM (C/C++) development. Digital IC Design and Embedded System Development. Independently developed projects include a parallel frequency-encoded readout architecture for iontronic sensor arrays, and the wireless integrated system for bioelectrical sensing, which incorporates electrical stimulation, biosignal acquisition, and bioelectrical impedance tomography (EIT).

  • Proficient in deep learning algorithms and programming, including transfer learning, and reinforcement learning. Integrated large-scale body-sensing signals with deep learning to achieve accurate prediction of user movement intentions and recognition of touch gesture commands.
