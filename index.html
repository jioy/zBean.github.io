Ongoing Research

Here, we present a walking collaborative robot equipped with multifunctional iontronic skins (named IM-Robot) that is capable of independent mobility skills (IMSs) assistance under real-time distributed tactile sensing, intelligent inference, and feedback control. Highly sensitive bimodal iontronic arrays (768 pressure pixels and 75 temperature pixels), connected by a network of conductive thread electrodes that are passively probed, are embedded on the robotic arm's surface to serve as distributed pressure and temperature sensing skins. The back-end interface design based on frequency-encoding architecture ensures high-speed, crosstalk-free parallel readout of iontronic arrays.
